I tried to survive on instant noodles for a month. By day three, I was so hungry I started eating the soup packets like they were snacks. I mean, they’re not even snacks—they’re just sad, pre-packaged disasters. But hey, at least I never had to worry about the noodles getting cold. I kept them in my pocket, and if I felt like I was about to die of starvation, I’d just microwave them and pretend I was in a survivalist camp. Turns out, the real survival skill is not the noodles, but the ability to convince yourself that a cup of broth and a plastic spoon is a gourmet meal. And if you’re lucky, you’ll find a nearby tree to use as a windbreak while you chew.
